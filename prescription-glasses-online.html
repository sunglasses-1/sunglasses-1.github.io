【The Ultimate Eyeglass Buying Guide for Online Shoppers】

Before You Order: What Do I Need to Order Glasses Online?

Step 1: Get a Current Prescription

- The Rule: The FTC’s Eyeglass Rule requires your eye doctor to provide you with a copy of your prescription upon request.

- Accuracy Check: An expert from the American Academy of Ophthalmology warns, "The wrong prescription can lead to headaches, eye strain or even nausea." This is crucial for determining if are online glasses prescriptions accurate.

Step 2: How to Measure Pupillary Distance (PD) at Home

- What it Is: PD is the distance between the centers of your pupils, which is essential for aligning your lenses correctly.

- How to Get It: While your doctor provides the most accurate number, many sites like Zenni and Warby Parker have digital tools to help you measure your PD with a webcam and a credit card for scale.

Step 3: How to Read Eyeglass Prescription

- Key Terms: Understand what OD (right eye), OS (left eye), SPH (sphere), CYL (cylinder for astigmatism), and AXIS mean to ensure you're entering your data correctly.

Choosing Your Lenses and Frames Like a Pro

Lens Types: Single Vision, Bifocal, and Progressive

- Single Vision: Corrects for one field of vision, often used to correct best online glasses for astigmatism.

- Progressive: Multifocal lenses with a seamless transition between distance, intermediate, and near vision. Panel testers found Frames Direct to have one of the "best fields of vision" for best online glasses for progressive lenses.

Lens Materials and Coatings: What to Choose

- High-Index Lenses Online: If you have a strong prescription, these lenses are thinner, lighter, and more cosmetically appealing. Most top retailers offer them as an upgrade.

- Blue Light Filtering Glasses: An optional coating that can help reduce eye strain for those who spend long hours in front of digital screens.

How to Choose Eyeglass Frames for Your Face

- Use the Tools: Leverage the virtual glasses try-on features offered by most sites. Upload a photo or use a live webcam to see how frames look.

- Filter Smartly: Narrow your search by filtering for frame width, shape, material, and color to find the perfect match.
